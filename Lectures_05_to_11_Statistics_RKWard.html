4 📘 Lecture 05: Essential Math for Statistics

This lecture lays the foundation of mathematical concepts essential to understanding statistics. Topics include coordinate geometry, linear and exponential functions, and basics of calculus.

4.1 📌 Key Concepts

4.1.1 📐 Number Line & Coordinate Geometry

  • A number line represents a variable’s values.
  • Cartesian plane: X-axis (independent), Y-axis (dependent).
  • Linear equation: \(Y = mX + C\), where:
    • \(m = \frac{Y_2 - Y_1}{X_2 - X_1}\) (slope)
    • \(C\) is the Y-intercept (Y when X = 0)

4.1.2 📊 Exponential Functions

  • Form: \(Y = a^X\) or \(Y = e^X\) (where \(e \approx 2.718\))
  • Models rapid, nonlinear growth.

4.1.3 🧮 Differentiation

  • Measures instantaneous rate of change: \[ \frac{dY}{dX} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(X + h) - f(X)}{h} \]

4.1.4 📈 Integration

  • Calculates area under the curve: \[ \bar{X} = \int X \cdot f(X) \, dX \]

5 📘 Lecture 06: Measures of Central Tendency

Central tendency summarizes a dataset with a single representative value.

5.1 📌 Definitions

  • Mean: Arithmetic average; sensitive to outliers.
  • Median: Middle value; robust to outliers.
  • Mode: Most frequent value; best for categorical data.

5.2 🔍 Characteristics

  • Simple, uses all values (mean).
  • Median is stable against extreme values.
  • Mode is useful for nominal data.

5.3 💡 Suitability by Data Type

Measure Data Type
Mean Interval/Ratio
Median Ordinal
Mode Nominal

5.4 📉 Skewness

  • Positive skew: Mean > Median
  • Negative skew: Mean < Median

6 📘 Lecture 07: Measures of Variability

Describes the spread of data.

6.1 📌 Measures

  • Range: Max - Min
  • Interquartile Range (IQR): \(Q_3 - Q_1\)
  • Variance: Avg. of squared deviations
  • Standard Deviation (SD): \(\sqrt{\text{Variance}}\)
  • Coefficient of Variation (CV): \(\frac{SD}{Mean}\)

6.2 📐 Theoretical Moments

Moment Interpretation
1st Mean
2nd Variance
3rd Skewness
4th Kurtosis

7 📘 Lecture 08: Mean-Centered & Standardized Data

Transformation techniques for advanced analysis.

7.1 🧮 Mean-Centering

  • Subtract mean from each data point.
  • New mean becomes zero.

7.2 📊 Standardization (Z-Score)

  • Formula: \(Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma}\)
  • Mean = 0, SD = 1
  • Unitless; allows comparison across distributions.

7.3 📌 Applications

  • Normalize different scales
  • Preprocessing in statistics and ML

8 📘 Lecture 09: Introduction to Probability

Foundation of inferential statistics.

8.1 📌 Key Concepts

  • Experiment: Controlled process
  • Event: Subset of outcomes
  • Sample Space (S): All possible outcomes

8.2 📈 Basic Rule

  • \(P(A) = \frac{\text{Favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total outcomes}}\)

8.3 🧠 Properties

  • \(0 \leq P(A) \leq 1\)
  • \(P(S) = 1\)
  • \(P(A^c) = 1 - P(A)\)
  • If \(A \subseteq B\), then \(P(A) \leq P(B)\)

8.4 🧮 Visual Aids

  • Venn diagrams
  • Frequency ↔︎ Probability distribution linkage

9 📘 Lecture 10: Distributions & Binomial Distribution

Probability distributions describe variable behavior.

9.1 🎲 Bernoulli Trials

  • Success (p) or failure (1 – p)

9.2 📊 Binomial Distribution

  • \(n\) independent trials
  • \[ P(X = r) = \binom{n}{r} p^r (1-p)^{n-r} \]

9.3 📈 Properties

  • Mean: \(np\)
  • Variance: \(np(1-p)\)

9.4 🎮 Tools

  • Galton board
  • Pascal’s triangle

10 📘 Lecture 11: Normal Distribution

Crucial distribution in statistics.

10.1 📌 Characteristics

  • Bell-shaped, symmetric about \(\mu\)
  • Area under curve = 1
  • Emerges with large samples

10.2 📉 Equation

\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \]

10.3 🧮 Z-Score

  • \(Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma}\)

10.4 🔍 Empirical Rule

Range Coverage
±1 SD 68%
±2 SD 95%
±3 SD 99.7%

10.5 📌 Applications

  • Heights, test scores, measurement errors
